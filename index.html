About

I am a senior at Miami University, where my studies in political science, philosophy, and economics have converged into a fascination with the "micro-to-macro" problem: how individual behavioral tendencies and personal biases aggregate into the complex, often unpredictable outcomes of dynamical systems. While my background began with foundational questions of coordination and legitimacy, I have since transitioned toward a technical framework utilizing mean field games, game theory, and rigorous mathematical modeling to explore why systems like financial markets and political landscapes fail to process information efficiently. My research approach is grounded in high-dimensional econometrics and computational modeling, focused on building modular pipelines that translate unstructured data into reproducible insights. Ultimately, I aim to apply this toolkit in a professional research setting as a pre-doctoral assistant before pursuing a PhD to further develop the methodological tools necessary for navigating our increasingly interconnected, high-dimensional world.
